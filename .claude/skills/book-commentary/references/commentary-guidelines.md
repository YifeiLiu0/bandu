# A3 and B3 commentary guidelines

## Product boundary

Optimize for:

1. `understandability`: professional content becomes easier to understand.
2. `continuation_desire`: the reader continues for a real knowledge reason.
3. `readable_rhythm`: prose scans easily and sounds natural aloud.
4. `cognitive_payoff_density`: every eligible beat delivers one specific understanding reward.

Use this principle:

```text
thin hook, substantial content; fast feedback, unhurried thinking
```

Never trade accuracy for entertainment, blame the reader, fabricate emotions or author intent, hide a current explanation to force continuation, or create a generic cliffhanger.

## Upstream analysis is binding

Honor every embedded conflict, difficulty, goal, angle, payoff, emotional target, loop action, voice contribution, form, analogy decision, factual boundary, forbidden move, and maximum length.

Return to `$pre-analysis` instead of silently changing a plan. A polished comment that solves a different cognitive problem fails.

## Close before opening

Deliver the current payoff visibly before any bridge. A reader who stops after the comment must still have gained a mechanism, distinction, judgment, action, correction, or connection.

Realize `close_and_open` only when planned. Only mentor may carry the exact planned bridge after the payoff. Do not reveal the future answer, add a second loop, bridge across direct `beats[]` arrays, or use generic language such as “接下来怎么办” without naming the precise missing relationship.

Reject teaser phrases such as “真正精彩的还在后面” or “事情没有这么简单”.

## Readable rhythm and form

- Give each sentence one job: expose friction, cross one reasoning step, or land the payoff.
- Prefer short spoken sentences and one- or two-sentence paragraphs.
- Use line breaks to expose structure, not to disguise dense prose.
- Preserve one dominant planned presentation form.
- Use `arrow_chain` only for at least three meaningful nodes across two transitions.
- Use `bullet_breakdown` only for genuinely parallel source items.
- Keep a core question concrete and answerable.
- Make mini-dialogue expose a real collision; never split a definition between speakers.

Do not replace the planned form merely for variety. When the planned form cannot truthfully represent the source, return to `$pre-analysis`.

## Plain language and analogy

Prefer people, roles, objects, actions, and consequences over abstract-noun chains. Explain an essential source term inline on first use, then reuse it consistently.

Use an analogy only when the plan enables it. Preserve the planned domain and exact mapping, keep it shorter than the explanation, and reconnect it explicitly to the source. Never introduce analogy details that could be mistaken for book claims.

## A3 structural prose

Build the opening from the precise collision in `conflict`. Promise only the understanding supported by `reward_design.promised_payoff` and its early checkpoint. Keep the answer hidden without hiding what the unit is for.

Realize the planned structural voice, opening angle, form, tone, constraints, and analogy. The opening should feel like a story beginning or a live question, not a lesson heading.

Make the closing question perform the planned exit function:

- ask for a tiny diagnosis;
- distinguish two nearby ideas;
- recover a mental model;
- apply the idea to a small scenario; or
- bridge only when the plan supports a real dependency.

Reject school-quiz wording, formal recap, unsupported future promises, and hooks whose first payoff arrives later than the planned checkpoint.

Default maximums come from the plan; for Chinese they are normally 90 characters for opening and 60 for closing. Stop earlier when the function lands.

## B3 six-pass drafting

For each planned voice, silently complete:

1. `payoff skeleton`: state the exact planned payoff without style devices.
2. `cognitive turn`: move from old belief through tension and information turn to payoff.
3. `entry and shape`: realize the planned human anchor, reader reaction, strategy, lively turn, voice, and form.
4. `close before open`: land the voice contribution and current payoff before any allowed mentor bridge.
5. `remove lecture scaffold`: hide taxonomy, labels, definitions, recaps, and teacherly prompts.
6. `spoken compression`: keep one source-bound device, split overloaded sentences, remove duplication, and stop on the landing.

Reject a draft when a later pass makes the payoff harder to recover.

## Student voice

Sound like a perceptive, funny, slightly sharp friend. Move from a concrete source cue through a mismatch or awkward consequence to a compact punchline, deadpan relabeling, or plain-language verdict.

- Roast the situation, system behavior, inflated promise, or harmless first impulse—not the reader's intelligence.
- Let the reaction carry its contribution; do not append a definition, takeaway, or study question.
- Make the roast source-specific enough that it cannot move unchanged to another beat.
- Treat first person as optional. Reject recurring `我以为……结果……懂了……` scaffolds.
- Keep student bridge-free.

## Mentor voice

Sound like a smart friend leaning over to point out the interesting part. Begin from a recognizable action, result, predicament, or contrast; expose the mistaken model; bridge the missing step; land on the exact source concept.

For medium/hard beats, directly resolve every label in `target_difficulty_labels`. Spend only the space needed for the missing reasoning bridge. Prefer several short lines over a complete-looking paragraph. Do not open with taxonomy or formal conclusion.

## Pair complementarity

When both voices appear:

- mentor makes the mechanism, distinction, or boundary easier to understand;
- student makes the source-specific mismatch or consequence easier to recognize and remember;
- neither paraphrases the other;
- each realizes its own contribution, human anchor, and landing;
- the final pair matches `pairing_design.non_overlap`.

Reject a student comment that is merely a shorter mentor comment with slang.

## Chapter-level variety

Keep a private ledger per voice with presentation form, line count, sentence shape, device, analogy domain, opening pattern, roast target, landing, and core claim. Inspect the previous three to five same-voice entries and the current pair.

Re-audit when:

- one form appears three times consecutively;
- schematic forms or opened bridges exceed roughly one third of eligible beats;
- adjacent comments both open loops;
- student comments reuse the same first-person scaffold;
- either voice covers under 70 percent of chapter beats.

Treat style thresholds as review triggers. Preserve source fit instead of rotating forms or jokes mechanically.

## Failure patterns

Reject prose that:

- summarizes instead of delivering the planned payoff;
- exposes planning labels or uses label-colon definitions;
- sounds like documentation, a lecture, or a quiz;
- uses a movable joke, invented scene, false causality, or unsupported author intent;
- repeats the conclusion in more formal language;
- promises a late/unsupported payoff or opens an unplanned loop;
- uses humor that obscures the reasoning bridge;
- exceeds the planned maximum;
- duplicates the paired voice.

## Acceptance tests

Run all applicable tests before setting self-check fields:

1. Grounding: every claim and device maps to source or plan.
2. Analysis alignment: prose cashes out the exact conflict/difficulty and contribution.
3. Beginner clarity: a zero-background reader can recover the relationship.
4. Human entry: the planned observable anchor or reaction becomes visible.
5. Payoff: the planned understanding gain lands before any bridge.
6. Competence: difficulty feels locatable and workable without blame or flattery.
7. Loop: bridge matches the planned dependency and checkpoint; no fake cliffhanger.
8. One job: the comment performs one primary cognitive change.
9. Form fit: visible shape matches the planned form and clarifies the relationship.
10. Analogy: when planned, mapping is exact; when unplanned, none appears.
11. Companion voice: prose sounds beside the reader, not above them.
12. Lecture removal: unnecessary taxonomy and teaching scaffold are gone.
13. Student roast: student text lands a source-specific roast without first-person dependence.
14. Pair complementarity: paired texts make distinct contributions.
15. Lookback: recent entries do not repeat the same delivery combination without source need.
